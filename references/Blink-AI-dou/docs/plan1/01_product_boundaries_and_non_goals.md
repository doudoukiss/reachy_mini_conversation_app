# Product Boundaries and Non-Goals

## Near-term north star

Blink-AI should become the software equivalent of a modern JARVIS-like companion:
- private by default
- present across terminal, voice, and screen
- persistent over time
- aware of local context
- capable of bounded actions
- calm, intelligent, and socially coherent

## What must be world-class

### 1. Daily usability
The system must be usable every day from a Mac without hardware attached.

### 2. Low-friction interaction
Terminal-first is acceptable and can be excellent.
The user should not need a browser unless they want inspection or approvals.

### 3. Continuity
Blink-AI should remember preferences, unfinished threads, recurring topics, and recent context.

### 4. Safe action
Digital actions should be deliberate, inspectable, and approval-aware.

### 5. Embodiment readiness
The body should consume semantic actions, not define the brain architecture.

## Non-goals for the next phase

Do not prioritize:
- new robot-only behaviors without stronger companion intelligence
- larger expression catalogs before product identity is clear
- more demo polish without daily usability gains
- more connector breadth before action quality is reliable
- local-only purity at the cost of conversational fluidity

## Product hierarchy

### Primary
Personal local companion.

### Secondary
Voice-capable desktop assistant.

### Tertiary
Embodied desk companion / expressive robot head.

### Quaternary
Venue concierge demo mode.

## Design tone

Blink-AI should feel:
- contemporary
- calm
- competent
- warm but not clingy
- technically serious
- emotionally legible

Avoid:
- theatrical sci-fi imitation
- over-eager proactive interruptions
- fake omniscience
- exaggerated robot affect
